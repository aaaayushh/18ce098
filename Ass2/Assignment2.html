The outbreak was first identified in Wuhan, Hubei, China, in December 2019 and recognised as a pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO). Regions affected by major outbreaks include mainland China, Europe, Iran, South Korea, and the United States.

The virus primarily spreads between people in a similar manner to influenza, via respiratory droplets from coughing or exhaling.The time between exposure and symptom onset is typically five days, but may range from two to fourteen days. Common symptoms include fever, cough and shortness of breath.
